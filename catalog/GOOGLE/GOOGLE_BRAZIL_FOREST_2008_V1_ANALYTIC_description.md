The Analytic Basemap of the Brazil Forest Imagery Dataset 2008 (G-BFC v1.0) provides a 10-meter resolution multispectral basemap (Green, Red, and Near-Infrared) synthesized from SPOT 2, 4, and 5 sensors. Unlike the Visual Basemap, this product provides Top-of-Atmosphere (TOA) reflectance data, designed specifically for scientific analysis, land cover modeling, and automated forest code compliance. The inclusion of the Near-Infrared (NIR) band enables the calculation of vegetation indices such as NDVI to identify "consolidated areas" (land deforested prior to the July 22, 2008 deadline).

The analytic product was generated by converting raw digital numbers to TOA reflectance using band-specific physical gain and bias metadata. Radiance values were normalized for solar irradiance, sun zenith angle, and Earth-Sun distance for each acquisition day. To minimize radiometric variance across the national-scale mosaic, the dataset underwent an advanced histogram matching workflow against a 2008 Landsat target, excluding water bodies and high-change pixels from the statistical baseline.

The dataset utilizes manual cloud and shadow masking to ensure the highest possible fidelity for quantitative modeling. Spatial accuracy is maintained through an automated displacement-based coregistration against Landsat Collection 2 references. The final mosaic prioritizes the highest resolution pixels available, layering 10m SPOT 5 multispectral data above 20m SPOT 4 and 2 data where concurrent coverage exists.

Gaps in coverage, primarily caused by persistent cloud obstructions during the 2007–2009 temporal window, are left transparent. The dataset primarily covers the 2008 calendar year but utilizes a 2007–2009 acquisition period where necessary to maximize coverage for high-priority rural properties (CAR) and forest credit marketplace verification (CRA).
