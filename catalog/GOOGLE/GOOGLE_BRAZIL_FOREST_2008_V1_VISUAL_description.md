The Brazil Forest Imagery Dataset 2008 (G-BFC v1.0) provides a 5-meter resolution visual imagery basemap of Brazil, synthesized from SPOT 2, 4, and 5 satellite observations. This dataset was produced by Google to establish a high-resolution terrestrial baseline for July 22, 2008—the regulatory deadline for identifying "consolidated areas" under the Brazil Forest Code. The mosaic represents a significantly higher resolution alternative to the 30-meter Landsat archive traditionally used for forest code compliance.

To maintain radiometric consistency, Google performed radiometric normalization on the source SPOT scenes via histogram matching against a 2008 Landsat reference mosaic. This workflow minimizes "checkerboarding" effects, ensuring seamless transitions between disparate satellite scenes. Additionally, an automated coregistration process using the `ee.Image.displacement` algorithm was applied to align SPOT imagery with the Landsat geographic baseline, correcting for significant misregistration in the source data.

The processing methodology includes manual cloud masking, where trained surveyors delineated polygons around clouds and shadows to ensure pixel reliability. Edge refinement via a 2.5-pixel focal minimum erosion was applied to image masks to mitigate lossy compression artifacts. For the Visual Basemap, pansharpened multispectral natural color (PMS_NC) data was prioritized for its 5m resolution, with native multispectral (MS_NC) data serving as a secondary source.

Geographic coverage was strategically prioritized for privately held lands registered in the Rural Environmental Registry (CAR), with a specific focus on the Amazon biome and priority states in the "Arc of Deforestation" (Maranhão, Mato Grosso, Pará, Rondônia, and Tocantins). In regions where high-quality 2008-era imagery was unavailable due to persistent cloud cover, gaps are represented by a transparent mask to maintain data integrity.
